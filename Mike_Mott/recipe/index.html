Eat: Chocolate P.B. Cup Cookies

Ingredients

  • 1 cup butter
  • 3/4 cup creamy peanut butter
  • 3/4 cup white sugar
  • 3/4 cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 1/3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/3 cup cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 cup semisweet chocolate chips
  • 1 cup peanut butter chips
  • 10 chocolate covered peanut butter cups, cut into eighths

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  2. In a large bowl, cream together the butter, peanut butter, white sugar, and brown sugar until smooth.
  3. Beat in the eggs one at a time, then stir in the vanilla. Combine the flour, cocoa, and baking soda; stir into the peanut butter mixture.
  4. Mix in the chocolate chips, peanut butter chips, and peanut butter cups.
  5. Drop by tablespoonfuls onto ungreased cookie sheets.
  6. Bake for 8 to 10 minutes in the preheated oven. Let cool for 1 or 2 minutes on sheet before removing, or they will fall apart.

Nutritional information

  • Calories: 230 | Total Fat: 13g | Cholesterol: 26mg

Drink: Decadent Hot Chocolate

Ingredients

Taken from The New York Times' Dining and Wine section, this recipe by Melissa Clark makes a delicious hot cocoa.

  • 3 ounces bittersweet chocolate, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2 tablespoons sugar, or to taste
  • 3/8 teaspoon ancho chile powder, or to taste
  • 1 3/4 cups whole milk
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Marshmallows or whipped cream, optional

Instructions

  1. In a medium pot, whisk together chocolate, cocoa, sugar and chile.
  2. Place pan over medium-low heat and whisk until chocolate begins to melt.
  3. Slowly whisk in milk, then cream. Drop in cinnamon stick. Bring to a simmer.
  4. Remove pot from heat. Cover pot tightly and steep 1 hour.
  5. Whisk in vanilla and taste for sweetness, adding more sugar if you like.
  6. Warm over low heat before straining and serving hot, with marshmallows or whipped cream if desired.
  7. Not from NYTimes: Decide whether you want to spike your creation with: rum, cinnamon liqueur or kahlua.
