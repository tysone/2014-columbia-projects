The J-School Hunger Games

Deadlines, deadlines and more deadlines! If the dark circles and limp, unwashed hair are not a hint, your life is pretty much over. Bad coffee, nicotine and a diet of chips are probably making it worse.

All you need are a couple of steps outside and you don't even have to cross the road. You will find a world on your plate- quite literally so! Cheap food with amazing conversation.

Meet the life-savers:

Know Your Food Carts

    Bangladesh

"I love it during the weekends when students come to me for food after midnight, completely drunk. I enjoy seeing their antics."

Mamun
came from Dhaka 8 years ago. He was trained as a typographer in his father's printing business but since he had no certificates his dream of being a web designer could never come true in the US. He works from 7- 2 am at Khan's gyro truck and makes enough to send home to his wife and his twin daughters.

His specialty: Lamb over Rice



India



"I love it when Indian students come to my cart. I am an Indian, so these students ask if I am from India and we talk. It feels nice."

Swamy
came from Chennai, India 8 years ago. A hotelier by profession he had a restaurant business back home. A single man, he loves the New York life.

His specialty: Mysore Masala Dosa

China



"The best part about putting my cart here is that there are many Asian students here. So the business is good. It's also easy to know if some of them are from my region in North China because of their accent. It's nice to meet people from my area."

Peter
and his brother came from North China to the US a few years ago. Putting up a food cart in the US they say is easier than in China. Business has been brisk and the lines are long as they cater to the hundreds of Asian students of the campus.

Their Speciality: Lunch Box with three dishes
