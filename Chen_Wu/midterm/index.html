The Empire State Building
The building you can never miss in New York City.


By Chen Wu
The Empire State Building at sunset. Photo: S J Pinkney

L ocated in Midtown Manhattan, at the intersection of Fifth Avenue and West 34th Street, the Empire State Building is one landmark that you will never miss in New York City.

According to Wikipedia, completed in early 1931, this 103-story skyscrpaer is the fourth-tallest completed skyscraper in the United States, and the 23rd-tallest in the world. As an American cultural icon, the building's name is derived from New York's nickname.
Empire State Building in construction.
Photo: Hine, Lewis Wickes, 1874-1940
Designed by William F.Lamb, and the site was first developed as the John Thompson Farm in the late 18th century. On January 22, 1930, the site was excavated and on March 17, the construction of the building started.

The constrction involved 3,400 workers, mostly immigrants from Europe. According to offcial accounts, five workers died during the construction and it cost 40,948,900 to build the building.
Workers on Empire State building.
Photo: Hine, Lewis Wickes, 1874-1940
At 9:40 am on Saturday, July 28, 1945, a B-25 Mitchell bomber, piloted by Lieutenant Colonel William Franklin Smith Jr. in thick fog, crashed into the north side of the Empire State Building between the 79th and 80th floors. Fourteen people died in the accident.

With 6,500 windows and 73 elevators, it was the first building to have more than 100 floors. The building houses 1,000 businesses and as of 2007, approximately 21,000 employees work there each day.

S ince 1931, the Empire State Building has been an international icon and has been immortalized on the Silver Screen as a timeless classic for not only New York, but the world. As a hot destination for Hollywood, filmmakers have featured the building in more than 250 feature films to tell their tales. Some of the most memorable Empire State Building moments can be seen in "King Kong," "An Affair to Remember," "Sleepless in Seattle," "Elf," and "Percy Jackson and the Olympians: The Lightning Thief." ---The Offcial Site of the Emipre State Building

A ccording to Wikipedia, in 1964, floodlights were added to illuminate the top of the building at night, in colors chosen to match seasonal and other events, such as St. Patrick's Day, Christmas and Independence Day.

Traditionally, the building will be lit in the colors of New York's sports teams on the nights they have home games. The building is illuminated in tennis-ball yellow during the US Open tennis tournament in late August and early September.

On November 6, 2012, CNN used the top of the Empire State Building as a scoreboard for the United States presidential election. When Barack Obama had reached the 270 electoral votes necessary to win re-election, the lights turned blue.
In the run-up week to Super Bowl XLVIII held at MetLife Stadium on February 2, 2014, the building was lit according to the amounts of Twitter hashtag, either green and navy blue of the Seattle Seahawks or orange and blue of the Denver Broncos.
