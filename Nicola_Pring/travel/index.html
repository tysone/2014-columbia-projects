GREAT ESCAPES

Backpack Southern Europe


There are few adventures more thrilling than packing your belongings in a backpack and exploring the world. From Spain to Italy, from Croatia to Cyprus, here are 10 of the best cities, islands and regions in Southern Europe and the Mediterranean to visit on a backpacking trip. Whether you travel to all of them, or choose just a few, you're sure to find fascinating ancient cities, miles of pristine beaches and incredible adventures. Go on, get out there.




1. Madrid, Spain

Explore the royal history of the Spanish empire in Spain's capital city. There are hundreds of cultural sights, museums and landmarks to see, and great neighborhoods and restaurants to explore. Madrid is also a great city for nightlife. It is populated with countless bars and clubs, and locals are known to stay out until early morning.

What To See

The Palacio Royal, (Royal Palace) the largest royal palace in Western Europe. Plan a visit to the beautiful palace, but don't expect to any royals. Spanish king Juan Carlos I doesn't actually live there.

Where To Stay

Hostal San Isidro in the center of the city. Room rates begin at about $40 a night.

What To Eat

Tapas. Try Spain's signature small plates. Check out The Expeditioner's list of the top 10 tapas Bars in Madrid.



2. Valencia, Spain

An ancient city meets modern architecture in Valencia. Though this Spanish city was founded by the Romans in the first century B.C., the newly-built City of Arts and Science center is possibly its most distinguishing feature. In addition to the more modern features, Valencia is steeped in traditional Spanish culture, as evidenced by ancient walls and cathedrals.

What To See

The City of Arts and Sciences. This science and cultural center is one of Valencia's most recognizable landmarks. Inside you'll find a planetarium, an opera house, an open-air park and more.

Where To Stay

Purple Nest Hostel in central Valencia. Rooms begin at around $25 a night.

What To Eat

Paella. This rice dish originated here.


3. Marseille, France

Europe and North Africa meet in this French port city. Marseille, sometimes known as Port d'Afrique, is filled with old seaports, markets and great restaurants. The city is a great place to visit in the summer, as it is home to a number of summer events including dance and music festivals.

What To See

The Calanque de Sugiton. Take a trip just outside of the city to one of the area's most beautiful calanques, or coves. The inlet is open year-round for hiking and exploring.

Where To Stay

Hello Marseille Hostel in downtown Marseille. This hostel was built as a non-profit by experienced backpackers. Rooms start at about $24 year-round

What To Eat

Bouillabaisse. No trip to Marseille would be complete without a bowl of this French fish stew, which originated in the coastal city.


4. Sardinia, Italy

You won't find more beautiful beaches than those on Sardinia. This island is home to quaint Italian cities, pure blue waters and hiking trails. It's a perfect place to explore national parks, go horseback riding, or just lie on the beach. Sardinia may be as close to paradise as you'll ever get.

What To See

Poetto Beach, near the city of Cagliari. Spend a day sunbathing and swimming on this pristine beach.

Where To Stay

Hostel Marina in Cagliari. This hostel, built in an ancient building in the old city, is located near all of Cagliari's beautiful sights. Rooms begin at about $35 dollars during the summer.

What To Eat

Cheese. Specifically Pecorino Romano, a Sardinian specialty.


5. Rome, Italy

No European vacation is complete without a trip to Rome. The famous city built on seven hills is home to the Colosseum, the Trevi Fountain, Vatican City, the Spanish Steps and countless other wonders. Spend your days exploring the city on foot and enjoying all Italy's capital has to offer.

What To See

The Trevi Fountain. Toss a coin into this famous 18th century landmark.

Where To Stay

Alessandro Palace Hostel. This hostel is easily accessible from Termini train station. Rooms start at around $36 per night in the summer.

What To Eat

Pizza. Check out The Guardian's guide to the best pizza places in Rome.


6. Dubrovnik, Croatia

This old Croatian city on the Adriatic coast is one the Mediterranean's most beautiful. Dubrovnik is the perfect destination for those who enjoy exploring both cities and outdoor adventures. Visitors can walk along the ancient walls that ring the old city, relax on Croatian beaches and try cliff-jumping.

What To See

The ancient city walls. Walls have surrounded Dubrovnik since it was founded in the 8th century. Take in the view during a walk around the Old Town Dubrovnik walls.

Where To Stay

Fresh Sheets Hostel in Old Town. Rooms at the seaside hostel begin at about $50 per night.

What To Eat

Fish. Don't miss the fresh fish in this coastal city.


7. Athens, Greece

From the Acropolis, to the Ancient Agora, to the Temple of Zeus, ancient Greek culture is everywhere in Athens. This capital city, one of the world's oldest, also features the beautiful Parnitha National Park, beaches, theaters, shops and great nightlife. Explore Athens on foot—it's one of Europe's best for walking.

What To See

The Acropolis. Take a walking tour of the rocky outcrop in Athens. The Parthenon, the ancient temple of the goddess Athena, sits at the top.

Where To Stay

Pergamos Hotel in the center of the city. This hotel is within walking distance of the Acropolis and other great sights. Rooms begin at about $38 per night in the summer.

What To Eat

Greek salad. Made with fresh vegetables and feta cheese.


8. Santorini, Greece

The most beautiful sunsets you will ever see are in Santorini. This island, built on the cliffs surrounding a volcano, is home to beautiful beaches and stunning Greek architecture. Santorini is a perfect place for hiking, horseback riding, visiting wineries and enjoying summer weather.

What To See

Cinekamari. Spend a summer evening in beautiful Santorini at this open air cinema.

Where To Stay

Hotel Villa Manos. This quiet hotel, which offers air conditioning and a swimming pool, has rooms beginning around $50 per night in the summer months.

What To Eat

Traditional Greek food. Santorini is home to great restaurants serving fresh Mediterranean fare. Check out Condé Nast Traveller's guide.


9. Istanbul, Turkey

Take a trip to the center of culture, history and religion in Southeastern Europe. Istanbul is best known for religious landmarks including the Hagia Sohpia, Sultanahment Mosque and the Basilica Cisterna, as well as historical sights, and great shopping and restaurants. For those who want to get out of the city, many beautiful islands are a short trip from Istanbul.

What To See

The Sultanahmet District. Plan a walking trip through this historic district where you can visit the Hagia Sophia and the Topkapi Palace. Don't miss shopping at Istanbul's grand bazaar.

Where To Stay

Metropolis Hostel in Sultanahmet. This hostel is a short walk from Istanbul's major sites. Rooms start at about $25 during the summer.

What To Eat

Turkish Delight. Don't pass up this traditional candy while you are here. Check out Fodor's guide to must-try food in Istanbul.


10. Nicosia, Cyprus

This capital city of the gorgeous island of Cyprus is divided into two sections. Though political tensions divided Northern Cyprus from the Republic of Cyprus, the island is a great tourist destination for those seeking sunshine. Enjoy the beautiful beaches, museums and arts centers.

What To See

Hike Mount Olympus. Take a trip outside the city to Mount Olympus, the highest point on Cyprus.

Where To Stay

Averof Hotel. This small hotel is a short walk from the city center, and attractions including the Cyprus Museum and the National Theatre. Rooms start at about $75 per night during the summer.

What To Eat

Fresh vegetables. Though Cyprus is a small island, the Mediterranean climate is ideal for fruits and vegetables of all kinds to grow.
